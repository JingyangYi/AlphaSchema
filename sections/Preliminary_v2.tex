\section{Preliminary}
\label{sec:preliminary}

\subsection{Alpha Mining}

Consider \(N\) tradable assets observed over \(T\) trading dates. At date \(t\),
the market state is represented by \(X_t\in\mathbb{R}^{N\times D}\), where rows
index assets and columns index observable market features. An \emph{alpha factor}
is an executable mapping
\[
  f \colon X_{t-\tau+1:t} \;\longrightarrow\; v_t \in \mathbb{R}^{N},
\]
which maps a lookback window of length \(\tau\) to a cross-sectional signal
\(v_t\).

A factor pool \(\mathcal{F}=\{f_1,\ldots,f_K\}\) is combined by a downstream model
\(g\) to produce predictions \(\hat y_t=g(f_1(X_{t-\tau+1:t}),\ldots,
f_K(X_{t-\tau+1:t}))\). Alpha mining seeks a pool that maximizes a task objective
\(\mathcal{J}\), such as predictive correlation or portfolio utility:
\[
  \mathcal{F}^{*}
  = \arg\max_{\mathcal{F} \subseteq \mathcal{A}}\;
    \mathcal{J}\bigl(g(\mathcal{F}), Y\bigr),
\]
where \(\mathcal{A}\) is the candidate factor universe and \(Y\) denotes future
returns. Final evaluation therefore occurs on executable factors, but the search
algorithm still must choose how candidates are represented before execution.

We distinguish the \emph{execution object}, namely the runnable factor \(f\), from
the \emph{search object}, namely the representation used for generation,
selection, mutation, and scoring. Existing methods often use formulas, ASTs,
programs, or trajectories as search objects. These forms are close to execution,
but they bind feedback to implementation details and make it difficult to
attribute success or failure to stable trading mechanisms.

\subsection{Structured Semantic Plans}

\method{} instead searches over structured semantic plans, which describe a
candidate trading mechanism before code generation. A plan is a compact
specification with five fields:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt, nosep]
  \item \textbf{Event}: the market phenomenon, such as breakout, volume
        expansion, or volatility compression;
  \item \textbf{Context}: the reference state in which the event is
        interpreted, such as a recent extreme, VWAP region, or cross-sectional
        quantile;
  \item \textbf{Qualities}: confirmation or filtering conditions, such as
        volume confirmation, multi-horizon consistency, or outlier removal;
  \item \textbf{Direction}: the assumed implication, such as continuation,
        reversal, or range oscillation;
  \item \textbf{Output}: the signal emission form, such as a continuous
        score, event decay, or cross-sectional rank.
\end{itemize}

Thus, a plan specifies \emph{what} event is observed, \emph{where} it is
conditioned, \emph{which} confirmations are required, \emph{what} direction is
implied, and \emph{how} the signal is emitted. The formal notation for this
schema space is deferred to Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_schema_notation}.
